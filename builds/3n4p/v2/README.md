# V2 — partial success

Motor **spun** on sensorless ESC (V1 never did). [CAD](https://cad.onshape.com/documents/c89f2c87853c9f2774f38798/w/97a941b73ea3b6d03a2aeefd/e/ee342bf624cf2c4ee2937c15?renderMode=0&uiState=6a57b1c4ce1bb754973f165e)

vs V1: tighter air gap (&lt;~1 mm), thinner tooth tips (0.5 mm), stator/rotor fillets, base wire routing, **more turns** on the same 24 AWG spool.

## Field notes (first runs)

- **Start:** needed a **manual flick** every time — sensorless ESC would not self-start
- **Speed:** only ran reliably at **low throttle**; higher ESC speed was problematic
- **Noise:** loud while running — likely **loose M3 shaft** in bearings (~2.8 mm screw in ~2.99 mm bore)
- **Shutdown:** stopped on its own after **&lt;1 min** at what felt like normal speed (no obvious mechanical bind)
- **Heat:** coils were **warm/hot** after the run; nothing melted

Not a finished motor yet — proves the V2 electrical + geometry path works, but shaft fit, thermal headroom, and sensorless startup still need work.
